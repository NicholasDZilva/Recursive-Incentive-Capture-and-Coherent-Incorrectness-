# Recursive Incentive Capture and Coherent Incorrectness

**Incentive Topology, Drift Regimes, and the Limits of Embedded Correction in Agentic Systems**  
Nicholas D’Zilva | Invariant Systems | March 2026  

---

## Overview

This paper introduces a structural framework for understanding failure modes in advanced AI systems operating under sustained optimisation pressure.

Rather than treating alignment as a problem of value specification or behavioural deviation, this work reframes failure as a consequence of **correct optimisation within incomplete and recursively reinforced incentive structures**.

The paper is a companion to *Alignment Under Drift* and extends the framework through two primary contributions:

- **Recursive Incentive Capture** — the convergence of systems and their governance mechanisms toward a shared incentive topology  
- **Coherent Incorrectness** — a regime where systems improve across all observable metrics while drifting from external ground truth and long-term recoverability  

---

## Key Concepts

### Recursive Incentive Capture
Systems, evaluation frameworks, and governance structures converge toward the same optimised objective space due to shared incentives, feedback loops, and institutional alignment.

### Coherent Incorrectness
A system state in which:
- performance increases  
- internal consistency improves  
- confidence rises  

while:
- alignment with external reality degrades  
- recovery pathways collapse  
- structural error accumulates  

### Drift Monotonicity Regime
A set of conditions under which system drift becomes **effectively irreversible in practice**, even if not strictly monotonic in theory.

Defined by:
- temporal compression  
- recursive feedback integration  
- bounded correction capacity  
- irreversibility accumulation  

### Correction Independence Continuum
Correction is not binary (embedded vs independent), but exists along a gradient.  
Movement along this continuum directly affects a system’s ability to detect and reverse drift.

---

## Core Insight

> AI systems do not fail because optimisation breaks.  
> They fail because optimisation succeeds within structurally incomplete objective frames.
